## Edits

1. **§6 fifth principle** — "Relay enforcement is an optimization, never an
   assumption." Client-side verification from the signed log IS the security
   boundary; Buzz relay-side enforcement (instant revocation cutoff, gated
   merges, membership-checked writes, private-group read gating) is
   defense-in-depth that no client, bridge, or consumer may reason from.
   Enumerate what every consumer verifies from the signed log alone:
   attestation chains before trusting an author, approvals counted before
   treating a merge as approved, revoked keys filtered at read time,
   private-group enforcement treated as unverified until the deployment
   proves it. Cross-reference §1's draft-dependency/layering rule and the
   research aggregate.

2. **§4 training-pipeline consequence** — new paragraph placed after the
   "identity and signing are load-bearing" paragraph (it sharpens exactly
   that claim), before History access grants. State the asymmetry: an
   interactive client that briefly trusts a revoked agent shows a stale
   message; an extractor that trusts the relay bakes unattested trajectories
   into model weights. Record as **requirements** (not "should"): the
   extraction pipeline applies attestation-validity filtering itself, never
   assuming the relay did; every corpus row is labeled with attestation
   state at extraction time. Cross-reference the new §6.5.

3. **§9 decision row** — one row covering both edits: requested by root
   (directive 0FD60E31), verdict adopted; note that it elevates the research
   recommendation (README finding 5) from research advice to platform
   principle + stated requirement.
