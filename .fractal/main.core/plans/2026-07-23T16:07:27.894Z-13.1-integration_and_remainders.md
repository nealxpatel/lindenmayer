## Design reconciliation (verify ↔ kinds)

The verify design assumed verdict = `event.content` ∈ {approve, reject}. The
committed kinds model (`models.py`, kind 42041) uses a **`verdict` tag** with
`Literal["approve","reject"]` and an `e` tag pointing at the request. The
committed model wins. `count_approvals` therefore selects candidates by:
kind == 42041, `event.verify()` true, `e` tag == request.id, `verdict` tag
∈ {approve, reject} (anything else excluded). Verify may import
`kinds.constants` for kind numbers (one-way dep, no cycle) but stays off the
pydantic models — tag-level reads keep the §6.5 boundary self-contained.
Everything else in the verify child's design is adopted unchanged
(AttestationOutcome ordering, strict conditions grammar, latest-verdict
tie-break `(created_at desc, id asc)`, REVOKED-over-UNATTESTED extension,
filter_attested latency-caveat docstring).
